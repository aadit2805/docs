**Sift** is an academic planning tool for Texas A&M students. It synthesizes grade distributions, professor ratings, prerequisite chains, and your transcript to surface course recommendations and generate multi-semester degree plans you can actually trust.

## What Sift does

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Smart recommendations" icon="sparkles">
    Score every (course, professor) combo against your priorities — GPA, professor ratings, difficulty, requirements — and get a ranked list.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Multi-semester planner" icon="calendar-days">
    Generate four valid degree plan variants (fastest, highest GPA, balanced, best professors) honoring prerequisites and credit limits.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Transcript ingest" icon="file-arrow-up">
    Upload your unofficial transcript PDF and Sift extracts your completed coursework using Claude.
  </Card>

  <Card title="Degree progress" icon="graduation-cap">
    See remaining requirements computed against your major's degree plan, with equivalency mapping handled automatically.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Who it's for

Undergraduates in CSCE for V1, with the data pipeline designed to extend to other Texas A&M departments. If you've ever stared at Howdy and Anex tabs in parallel trying to decide which professor to take next semester, Sift is for you.

## How it works

1. **Sign in** with your account (Clerk handles auth).
2. **Upload** your transcript or pick completed courses manually.
3. **Set priorities** — do you optimize for GPA, finishing fast, easier professors, or a balance?
4. **Get a plan** — Sift returns four ranked plan variants and per-course recommendations.
