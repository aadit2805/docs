This guide walks you through using Sift for the first time — from sign-in to a generated multi-semester plan.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in">
    Open Sift and sign in with your account. Authentication runs through Clerk; we don't store your password.

    <Tip>
      Sift only protects routes that need your data (transcript, plans). Browsing courses and professors works without an account.
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Tell Sift what you've completed">
    You have two options:

    - **Upload your unofficial transcript PDF.** Sift extracts your completed courses using Claude. Larger transcripts may take a few seconds.
    - **Pick courses manually.** Useful if you'd rather not upload a transcript or want to model a hypothetical "what if I had taken X" scenario.

    Completed courses are saved to your profile so you only do this once.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your priorities">
    Tell Sift what matters to you. The recommendation engine weights five signals:

    | Signal | Default weight |
    | --- | --- |
    | Course GPA | 0.25 |
    | Professor rating | 0.20 |
    | Would-take-again | 0.15 |
    | Difficulty | 0.15 |
    | Requirement urgency | 0.15 |

    Tweak these to taste — for example, a student aiming to graduate fast can boost requirement urgency; a GPA-focused student can lean on course GPA and professor rating.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Get recommendations and a plan">
    Sift returns:

    - A **ranked list of (course, professor) combos** for next semester
    - **Four full multi-semester plans**: fastest, highest GPA, balanced, and best professors
    - **Killer-combo warnings** when the planner detects two notoriously brutal courses scheduled together

    Save your favorite plan and revisit it whenever you're choosing classes.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## What Sift won't do (yet)

- It doesn't enroll you. Use Howdy for registration — Sift is the planning layer.
- Section-level scheduling (avoiding 8 AMs, etc.) is disabled in V1 because we don't yet ingest section data.
- Non-CSCE majors are partially supported; the data pipeline expands by department.
